What the Permit Process Looks Like

  1. Pre-application meeting (optional but recommended)

    Schedule a 30-minute planning meeting at SF Planning to catch issues early. Free for ADUs.

  2. Hire a licensed architect or designer

    SF requires stamped architectural drawings for most ADU permits. Your designer prepares site plans, floor plans, and elevations.

  3. Submit to SF Planning Department

    Planning reviews for zoning compliance (setbacks, height, FAR). Most ADUs are over-the-counter (OTC) if they meet state ADU law.

  4. DBI building permit

    After Planning, submit structural, electrical, and mechanical plans to the Department of Building Inspection. Timeline: 2–6 months for in-house review.

  5. Construction + inspections

    DBI inspects at foundation, framing, rough-in, and final stages. Certificate of Occupancy issued after final inspection passes.
